Defense for Catan

Have you ever played Catan and Everyone is ganging up on you?
Then the probability of You Winning is probably Zero.

Try playing Defense for Catan

Rules

  • A fence or a wall will protect the player from the robber.
    (A player will still receive a resource where robber is if they build a fence or a wall)

  • Each player needs 15 pieces of walls same with roads.
    (Use popsicle sticks or cardboards use one side for fence and flip side for wall)

  • A fence will cost two woods.
    (Players can upgrade fence to a wall for greater protection)

  • A wall will cost a wood, a clay, and an ore.
    (Walls will be downgraded to a fence when the robber landed on that tile)

  • To upgrade a fence to a wall, a player can spend.
    a clay and an ore
    or
    two ores

  • Two new development cards wall building.
    Place two new fences as if you bought them.
    Or place single wall as if you bought a fence and upgraded it to a wall.
    Or upgrade two already existing fences to walls.
    Or place a new fence and upgrade a separate already existing fence to a wall.

  • When a seven is diced.
    The player with fence on the tile where the the robber lands won't have any cards stolen from hand but a fence stolen instead.
    (Place stolen fences on a dessert tile)
    The player with wall on the tile where the the robber lands will have their wall downgraded to a fence, no card will be stolen from hand.
    (Downgraded wall counts as one stolen fence)

  • Once the robber stole seven fences it learns the skill "wall jump".

  • Once the robber learns "wall jump".
    Fences will no longer be stolen.
    Fences can no longer protect the player and a resource card will be stolen from hand when robbed.
    Fences can no longer protect the player and will not receive a resource where robber is when diced.
    Walls can still protect the player, will be downgraded to fence when robbed and no card will be stolen from hand.
    Walls can still protect the player and will still receive a resource where the robber is when diced.

  • Players can use a knight card to move the robber and steal a fence.
    (Players can also use a knight card to move the robber and downgrade a wall)

  • A player can build or upgrade a fence where the robber is currently at.
    (This way the player can still get a resource where the robber is when diced)

  • A player with three walls (not fence) on a single settlement or city will earn the title "strong hold" for 2 victory points.
    (Strong hold settlement or city walls can no longer be downgraded nor cards stolen from hand)

  • Breaking the title "strong hold".
    The player with most walls will receive the title "strong hold" if players has equal number of strong holds.
    E.g. the player with two strong holds and three separate walls will beat the player with two strong holds and two separate walls.
    The player with more strong holds will receive the title "strong hold" than the player with more walls.
    E.g. the player with two strong holds will beat the player with one strong hold and 5 separate walls.
